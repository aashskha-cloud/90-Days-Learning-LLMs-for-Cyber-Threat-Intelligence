
# Week 1 Research Notes

## Research Topic

Large Language Models for Cyber Threat Intelligence: Challenges, Hallucinations, Trustworthiness and Future Directions

# Week 1 – Foundational Research Questions

## Question 1

### What is Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI), and how is it traditionally generated and used in cybersecurity?

Research Focus:

* Definition of CTI
* Strategic, Operational, Tactical, and Technical CTI
* How security analysts currently collect and analyze threat intelligence
* Challenges of manual CTI generation

Why this matters:
Before understanding how LLMs can improve CTI, you must understand how CTI is produced today.

---

## Question 2

### How are Large Language Models (LLMs) currently being applied to Cyber Threat Intelligence tasks?

Research Focus:

* Threat report summarization
* Malware and vulnerability analysis
* IOC extraction
* Threat actor profiling
* Security incident reporting

Why this matters:
This question helps identify where LLMs are already creating value in cybersecurity workflows.

---

## Question 3

### What are hallucinations in Large Language Models, and why are they particularly dangerous in cybersecurity applications?

Research Focus:

* Definition of hallucination
* Types of hallucinations
* Examples of false threat intelligence
* Impact on analyst decision-making
* Risks in Security Operations Centers (SOCs)

Why this matters:
Hallucinations are one of the biggest barriers to trustworthy AI in cybersecurity.

---

## Question 4

### What factors influence the trustworthiness and reliability of LLM-generated Cyber Threat Intelligence?

Research Focus:

* Accuracy
* Explainability
* Transparency
* Source attribution
* Confidence estimation
* Human-in-the-loop validation

Why this matters:
This question directly connects AI trustworthiness with cybersecurity requirements.

---

## Question 5

### What are the major open research challenges and future directions for LLM-based Cyber Threat Intelligence systems?

Research Focus:

* Hallucination mitigation
* Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
* Adversarial attacks
* Privacy concerns
* Evaluation benchmarks
* AI-assisted Security Operations Centers

Why this matters:
This question will eventually become the "Future Research Directions" section of your paper.
